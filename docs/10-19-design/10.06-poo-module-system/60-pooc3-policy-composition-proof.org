* Core Model

#+begin_src typst
$
"allows"("allOf"(p,q), c) & <=> "allows"(p,c) and "allows"(q,c) \
"allows"("anyOf"(p,q), c) & <=> "allows"(p,c) or "allows"(q,c) \
"allows"("override"("parent","local",o), c)
  & <=> (o(c) and "allows"("local",c))
      or (not o(c) and "allows"("parent",c)) \
"CanStart"("withPolicyObject"("spec",p), s, m)
  & <=> "StartFrame"("spec",s,m)
      and "allows"(p, "context"(s,m)) \
"StartFrame"("spec",s,m)
  & <=> "deps"("spec",s,m)
      and "scope"("spec",s,m)
      and "precondition"("spec",s,m)
      and "guard"("spec",s,m)
$
#+end_src

The key separation is that policy composition is POO-native, while
=StartFrame= keeps dependency, scope, precondition, and guard gates out of the
policy object itself.

* Policy Bundle Algebra

Single-object policy composition is not enough for POO. A realistic flow has a
family of policy objects, indexed by facet, and each module declares which
facets are required at startup.

#+begin_src typst
$
"bundle" & : "Facet" -> "PolicyObject"("Context") \
"requiresAt"("bundle", R, c)
  & <=>
    forall f, R(c,f) => "allows"("bundle"(f),c) \
"allows"("requirementsPolicy"("bundle",R), c)
  & <=> "requiresAt"("bundle",R,c) \
"bundleAllOf"(p,q)(f)
  & = "allOf"(p(f),q(f)) \
"overrideFacet"("parent","local",t,o)(f)
  & =
    cases(
    "override"("parent"(t),"local"(t),o) & f=t,
    "parent"(f) & f!= t
  )
$
#+end_src

* Start Contracts

The bundle proof is still too low-level for reviewing an agent-authored
workflow. The next proof surface turns =CanStart= into a module-specific
contract: dependency facts, the non-policy start frame, and required policy
facets are all projected out of one startup proof.

* Refinement

Refinement is the proof layer that prevents a local policy or agent-authored
override from silently broadening what may start.

#+begin_src typst
$
"refines"(p_s,p_b)
  & <=>
    forall c, "allows"(p_s,c) => "allows"(p_b,c) \
"refines"(B_s,B_b)
  & <=>
    forall f,c, "allows"(B_s(f),c) => "allows"(B_b(f),c) \
"refines"(B_s,B_b) and "CanStart"("uiBundleSpec"(B_s),s,m)
  & => "CanStart"("uiBundleSpec"(B_b),s,m)
$
#+end_src

For a selector override, the safety condition is local-to-parent refinement on
the selector facet. Under that condition, the override cannot enable any UI
module that the parent bundle would reject.

#+begin_src typst
$
(forall c, "allows"(L("select" or),c)
  => "allows"(P("select" or),c))
  & =>
    "refines"("overrideFacet"(P,L,"select" or,o),P) \
"CanStart"("uiBundleSpec"("overrideFacet"(P,L,"select" or,o)),s,m)
  & => "CanStart"("uiBundleSpec"(P),s,m)
$
#+end_src

* State Progression

Startup proofs are static unless state evolution is modeled. =UiStateExtends=
is the monotone lifecycle relation: active scope may grow, completed facts may
be added, and existing facts cannot regress.

#+begin_src typst
$
"extends"(s_0,s_1)
  & => "activeScope"(s_0) <= "activeScope"(s_1) \
"extends"(s_0,s_1)
  & => "completed"(s_0,m) => "completed"(s_1,m) \
"extends"(s_0,s_1)
  & => "precondition"(s_0,m) => "precondition"(s_1,m) \
"extends"(s_0,s_1)
  & => "guard"(s_0,m) => "guard"(s_1,m)
$
#+end_src

Policy facets are intentionally not assumed monotone for free. The proof asks
for a facet-stability witness at the module being preserved:

#+begin_src typst
$
"stable"(B,s_0,s_1,m)
  & <=>
    forall f, "required"(m,f) =>
      "allows"(B(f),"ctx"(s_0,m)) =>
      "allows"(B(f),"ctx"(s_1,m)) \
"extends"(s_0,s_1) and "stable"(B,s_0,s_1,m)
  and "CanStart"("uiBundleSpec"(B),s_0,m)
  & => "CanStart"("uiBundleSpec"(B),s_1,m)
$
#+end_src

For safe selector overrides, progression and refinement compose: an override
that was safe before the state extension remains reducible to the parent after
the state extension.

#+begin_src typst
$
"extends"(s_0,s_1) and "stable"("overrideFacet"(P,L,"select" or,o),s_0,s_1,m)
  & and "safeSelectorOverride"(P,L) \
  & and "CanStart"("uiBundleSpec"("overrideFacet"(P,L,"select" or,o)),s_0,m) \
  & => "CanStart"("uiBundleSpec"(P),s_1,m)
$
#+end_src

* Completion Trace

State progression says facts do not regress. A completion trace step says why a
new fact appears: a module was startable before the step, the state extended,
and the module is completed after the step.

#+begin_src typst
$
"CompletionStep"(B,s_0,s_1,m)
  & <=>
    "CanStart"("uiBundleSpec"(B),s_0,m)
    and "extends"(s_0,s_1)
    and "completed"(s_1,m) \
"CompletionStep"(B,s_0,s_1,m)
  & => "completed"(s_1,m) \
"CompletionStep"(B,s_0,s_1,m)
  and "completed"(s_0,d)
  & => "completed"(s_1,d)
$
#+end_src

The trace layer can now distinguish real unlocks from false unlocks. Completing
=strategyPlan= supplies the strategy dependency for =runtimeManifest=, but
runtime still needs validation, memory, compression, scope, and policy facets.
Benchmark remains blocked without =scenarioMatrix= even after strategy
completion.

#+begin_src typst
$
"CompletionStep"(B,s_0,s_1,"strategyPlan")
  and "validation"(s_1)
  and "memory"(s_1)
  and "compression"(s_1)
  and "runtimeScope"(s_1)
  and "runtimePolicy"(B,s_1)
  & => "CanStart"("uiBundleSpec"(B),s_1,"runtimeManifest") \
"CompletionStep"(B,s_0,s_1,"strategyPlan")
  and not "matrix"(s_1)
  & => not "CanStart"("uiBundleSpec"(B),s_1,"scenarioBenchmark")
$
#+end_src

* Control Plane

The session lifecycle is where POO policy composition can hide real bugs. A
session must not merely start; after startup, every tool call, file access, and
subagent launch must remain bounded by the session sandbox.

#+begin_src typst
$
"SessionProfile" & = brace.l "sandbox" brace.r \
"SandboxProfile" & = brace.l "toolAllowed", "fileAllowed", "capabilityAllowed" brace.r \
"Trace"("session","useTool"(t))
  & => "toolAllowed"("session"."sandbox",t) \
"Trace"("session","readFile"(f))
  & => "fileAllowed"("session"."sandbox",f) \
"Trace"("session","writeFile"(f))
  & => "fileAllowed"("session"."sandbox",f) \
"Trace"("session","startSubagent"(a,"child"))
  & =>
    forall c, "capabilityAllowed"("child"."sandbox",c)
      => "capabilityAllowed"("session"."sandbox",c)
$
#+end_src

The corresponding negative theorems are the important reflection surface:
denied tools, out-of-scope files, and subagent capability escalation cannot
produce a valid trace.

* Reference Boundary

External production systems are reference evidence, not verification evidence.
=OpenRATH= can suggest missing control-plane cases, but it cannot prove a POO
flow claim by itself.

#+begin_src typst
$
"PooFlowOwnedFact"(C) & => C \
"ReferenceObservation"("OpenRATH",C) & arrow.r.double.not C \
"VerifiedFromReference"(r) & => "AdoptionCertificate"(r) \
"AdoptionCertificate"(r) & => C
$
#+end_src

For control-plane traces, this means a production reference trace must be
adopted by the POO flow proof boundary before it can discharge tool, file, or
subagent capability obligations.

#+begin_src typst
$
"VerifiedFromReference"("OpenRATHTrace"("session","useTool"(t)))
  & => "toolAllowed"("session"."sandbox",t) \
"VerifiedFromReference"("OpenRATHTrace"("session","readFile"(f)))
  & => "fileAllowed"("session"."sandbox",f) \
"VerifiedFromReference"("OpenRATHTrace"("session","startSubagent"(a,"child")))
  & =>
    forall c, "capabilityAllowed"("child"."sandbox",c)
      => "capabilityAllowed"("session"."sandbox",c)
$
#+end_src

Negative reflection is explicit: if the POO flow sandbox denies the tool, file,
or child capability, an OpenRATH reference cannot be adopted as a verified POO
trace for that event.

* Reference Audit
Reference systems can create candidate obligations. They do not close the proof. The obligation must either be discharged by a POO-owned fact or by a verified adoption certificate; otherwise the proof surface records a rejection certificate.
#+begin_src typst
$
"CandidateObligation"(S,C) & = "ReferenceObservation"(S,C) \
"ObligationDischarge"(o) & ::=
  "PooFlowOwnedFact"(C) | "VerifiedFromReference"(o."reference") \
"RejectionCertificate"(o) & => not "ObligationDischarge"(o) \
"AuditResult"(o) & ::= "discharged"(o) | "rejected"(o)
$
#+end_src

For control-plane reference traces, discharge is still checked against the POO sandbox and capability lattice.
#+begin_src typst
$
"discharged"("session","useTool"(t)) & => "toolAllowed"("session"."sandbox",t) \
"discharged"("session","readFile"(f)) & => "fileAllowed"("session"."sandbox",f) \
"discharged"("session","startSubagent"(a,"child"))
  & =>
    forall c, "capabilityAllowed"("child"."sandbox",c)
      => "capabilityAllowed"("session"."sandbox",c) \
not "toolAllowed"("session"."sandbox",t)
  & => "RejectionCertificate"("session","useTool"(t)) \
not "fileAllowed"("session"."sandbox",f)
  & => "RejectionCertificate"("session","readFile"(f))
$
#+end_src

The base control-plane event =sendToSubagent= is intentionally exposed as a gap: the current =eventAllowed= rule accepts it with =True=. That base trace is not enough to prove a lifecycle-safe message. The linked lifecycle model adds active-subagent, channel, and policy obligations.
